After the Meeting

After a meeting, visit or gathering, you should acknowledge the event by writing to the chief and council or other officials who were present. In the case of less formal gatherings, an email may suffice, depending on the community.

This will give you the opportunity to convey your impressions of the issues that were addressed and allow your contact to follow up with any questions they might have.

The email in particular will also provide a record of the gathering for future reference.

Final Thoughts

Keep in mind that there is a great deal of cultural diversity within Indigenous communities and that the examples provided in this document are not “carved in stone.”

By familiarizing yourself with the practices of the community you are visiting, you increase your level of understanding and respect for the relationships you wish to develop.

Speak the truth, be yourself, be in the moment—and enjoy the experience!
