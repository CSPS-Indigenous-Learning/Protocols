Glossary

  • Advise

    recommend a course of action; counsel; give advice (not simply to tell or inform)

  • Analyze

    study the factors of a situation/problem in detail to determine the solution/ outcome

  • Anticipate

    foresee events, trends, consequences or problems

  • Collaborate

    work with and act jointly with others

  • Consult

    confer, seek the advice of others

  • Control

    exercise direction, guidance or restraint over someone/something; check or regulate; keep within limits

  • Co-ordinate

    bring into common action; harmonize, integrate

  • Correlate

    connect systematically to establish a mutual or reciprocal relation

  • Develop

    evolve, promote or make active a plan, program or course of action, usually without benefit of prior conclusion or determination

  • Evaluate

    appraise; consider the value of

  • Examine

    investigate; scrutinize; subject to inquiry or inspection; test by an appropriate method

  • Formulate

    develop or devise a statement of policy or procedures; put in a systemized statement as in a statement of procedure

  • Initiate

    originate; introduce in the first instance; cause or bring to pass by original act as in originating a plan, policy or procedure

  • Participate

    to have a part or share in something (not a recommended word to use unless it is followed by a explanation of "how" the participation occurs followed by examples)

  • Plan

    devise or project a method or course of action

  • Propose

    offer for consideration or adoption; form or declare a plan or intention

  • Revise

    rework in order to correct or improve; make a new, improved or up to date version

  • Study

    ponder or fix the mind closely upon a subject; endeavor with forethought and planning; consider attentively; devise with deliberation

  • Survey

    determine and delineate the form, extent, position, etc. of a situation; (usually in connection with gathering of information)

  • Adopt

    take and apply or put into practice; accept (as a report or a recommendation)

  • Approve

    sanction officially; accept as satisfactory; ratify (thereby assuming responsibility)

  • Determine

    fix conclusively or authoritatively; decide; order; come to a decision as a result of investigation, reason, etc.; settle; give a definite direction or impetus to

  • Direct

    give an order or instruction to; cause the following of a course of action

  • Review

    go over or examine deliberately or critically (usually with a view to approval or dissent)

  • Act

    perform; do something; serve or operate in fulfillment of a special function

  • Administer

    manage or direct the execution, application or conduct of, as in administering a program

  • Aid

    help; further

  • Assemble

    collect or gather together in a predetermined order, fit together the parts of

  • Assign

    fix, specify or designate; prescribe (as in assigning responsibility)

  • Assist

    end aid; help; give support to

  • Assure

    make certain of

  • Audits

    determines adherence to policies/processes

  • Circulate

    pass into the hands of readers

  • Collect

    gather or assemble into one body or place usually for further study or examination

  • Compile

    collect (information); compose out of materials from other documents

  • Conduct

    carry on, have the direction of; direct the execution of

  • Disseminate

    spread information or ideas (usually in the sense of a diffuse or widespread audience)

  • Distribute

    apportion or deal out as in distributing literature or mail

  • Draft

    write or compose papers or documents in rough, preliminary or final form, usually for clearance and approval by others

  • Establish

    set up; institute; place on a firm basis

  • Execute

    give effect to, follow out or through to the end, as a purpose

  • Exercise

    bring to bear or employ actively (as in exercising authority or influence)

  • Facilitate

    make easy or, less difficult (usually by doing something to advance the accomplishment of an act)

  • Forward

    send or transmit onward (usually in the sense of forwarding from one point of activity to another)

  • Furnish

    provide what is necessary for; supply; give

  • Implement

    carry out; perform acts essential to the execution of a plan or program; give effect to

  • Inform

    tell or make known; communicate knowledge to; acquaint

  • Issue

    release or send out; publish

  • Maintain

    as in "maintains machinery in good working order"

  • Notify

    give notice to; inform or make known

  • Observe

    inspect or take note of; pay attention; perceive or notice

  • Obtain

    get hold of (by effort); gain possession of; acquire; get; secure

  • Operate

    conduct or perform an activity

  • Perform

    carry out or execute some action; carry out to the finish; accomplish

  • Provide

    supply for use; furnish; take precautionary measures in view of a possible need

  • Receive

    accept, get, or take what is delivered from another source

  • Refer

    send or direct to a person or place for action, approval, etc.

  • Release

    set free (as in releasing information); permit at a specified date but not before the publication or dissemination of

  • Secure

    get possession of; obtain

  • Select

    take by preference or choice from among others; pick out.

  • Stimulate

    impel; instigate; urge (usually in the sense of stimulating others who have primary responsibility)

  • Supervise

    oversee for direction; inspect with authority; guide and instruct with immediate responsibility for purpose of performance; superintend; lead

  • Transmit

    send or transfer from one person or place to another

  • Undertake

    take upon one's self; engage in; enter upon

  • Verify

    prove to be true or accurate; confirm or substantiate; check exactness

  • Sub

    A sub is an object from the menu, wether it it is a page or represents a section that contains other subs (be they more granular sections or pages themselves).
